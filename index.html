SuryaHub: Customer Behavior & Sales Analysis

SQL BigQuery Looker Studio Data Visualization

📋 Background

SuryaHub is a fast-growing e-commerce platform offering products across multiple categories, including food, household supplies, electronics, and luxury goods. This project focuses on analyzing customer transaction data to help the marketing team gain clear visibility into purchasing patterns, loyalty program effectiveness, and campaign performance — enabling data-driven decisions without solely relying on increased marketing budget.

📌 Scope of Work

  • •Analyzed customer transaction records using BigQuery SQL to uncover purchasing patterns and customer segments
  • •Queried gender distribution, top cities by volume, and top 10 highest-spending customers
  • •Evaluated loyalty program membership impact on purchase amount and frequency
  • •Analyzed purchase intent and channel performance to identify high-value customer segments
  • •Built an interactive Looker Studio dashboard to visualize demographics, geographic performance, and buying behavior

⚙️ Process

Started by exploring the SuryaHub dataset in BigQuery, writing targeted SQL queries to answer key business questions around gender distribution, regional performance, loyalty membership, purchase intent, and channel behavior. Each query was designed to surface a specific insight for the marketing team. Findings were then connected to Looker Studio to build an interactive dashboard covering customer demographics (age and gender distribution), geographic sales performance across the top 5 cities, loyalty program effectiveness, purchase intent segmentation, and the impact of social media on purchase behavior across channels.

🎯 Results

  • •Identified female customers (265 transactions) as the primary demographic segment to prioritize in marketing campaigns
  • •Mapped Makassar as the highest-revenue city (Rp421.1M), providing geographic focus for regional promotions
  • •Revealed that non-loyalty members outspend members in both value and frequency, signaling the need for loyalty program restructuring
  • •Found planned buyers to be the most valuable segment by average spend, with impulsive buyers as a high-potential growth target
  • •Delivered actionable recommendations for SuryaHub's growth strategy

Kopi Nangkau: Sales Performance Analysis

Python Pandas NumPy Matplotlib Seaborn Data Visualization

📋 Background

Kopi NangKau is a growing local coffee shop business that has collected daily transaction data from 2021 to 2023, covering a variety of beverage and food menu items. They want to optimize their business strategy, management needs deeper insights into best-selling products, sales trends, and future growth potential.

📌 Scope of Work

  • •Cleaned and processed 6,600 daily transaction records using Python (Google Colab), reducing the dataset to 6,041 rows after handling missing values and duplicates
  • •Engineered a Revenue (Pendapatan) column by multiplying units sold by unit price
  • •Analyzed total units sold and revenue per product across 2021–2023
  • •Identified best-selling products per year and monthly sales trends
  • •Conducted sales growth analysis to compare product performance between 2021 and 2023

⚙️ Process

Started with data exploration to identify quality issues, finding duplicates and missing values across the Produk, Unit_Terjual, and Harga_Per_Unit columns. Applied targeted imputation strategies — filling missing product names from price references and vice versa — then dropped rows where both were missing. Engineered a Revenue column and formatted dates into datetime for time-series analysis. Conducted product-level and monthly aggregations to surface sales trends, best-sellers, and year-over-year growth patterns.

🎯 Results

  • •Confirmed coffee as the dominant category, with Latte and Cappuccino generating the highest revenue and most stable sales trends across all three years
  • •Identified shifting best-sellers by year: Americano (2021), Cappuccino (2022), and Espresso (2023)
  • •Found total units sold declined slightly, while revenue continued to grow — indicating successful price optimization
  • •Detected monthly sales fluctuations with the highest peak in March 2022 (6,024 units) and the lowest in February 2022 (5,011 units)
  • •Flagged the bakery category (Croissant & Donut) as underperforming with a downward sales trend, warranting resource reallocation
